Why simple layouts are easier to trust

Good static pages do not need heavy decoration. Clear spacing, steady typography, and direct navigation already make content feel reliable.

When a layout is simple, the reader can focus on the message first. This is especially helpful in academic and portfolio work where the main goal is clarity. A quiet visual system also makes each section easier to scan.

Basic HTML structure becomes stronger when headings, paragraphs, and navigation work together in a predictable order. CSS then supports that structure through spacing, borders, and readable contrast.

The result is not empty or plain. It feels intentional. That is why minimal layouts are often a good choice for student websites.
